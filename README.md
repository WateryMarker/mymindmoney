#Abstract 
Computer Project on MindMyMoney. The project is guided by two main objectives: 
1) to develop a user-friendly and informative platform that teaches users about various types 
of loans and how to manage them, 2) to evaluate the effectiveness of the website in improving 
users' financial decision-making skills. Through this initiative, the project seeks to empower 
individuals especially students and young adults to make informed, data-driven loan decisions 
that contribute to long-term financial stability. The procedures were divided into 2 parts as 
follows: Part 1, designed by integrating real-life financial website with mathematics and using 
HTML, CSS, JavaScript and PHP programming. Part 2, to study user by satisfaction of the 
MindMyMoney website. The sample group consists of 30 teachers from Thatnaraiwittaya 
School and 30 parents of Thatnaraiwittaya School Students, selected by purposive sampling, 
The MindMyMoney website satisfaction questionnaire consisting. The questionnaire on Likert 
scale. The collected data was analyzed by mean, Standard Deviation (SD) and percentage. 
These statistical measures were used to compare the satisfaction levels among the users. 
The results showed that in Part 1, outcome demonstrates that combining mathematics 
with practical financial tools can create a powerful educational resource for promoting money 
literacy in an increasingly complex financial world and supports responsible financial 
planning. Part 2, the evaluation of user satisfaction for the MindMyMoney website, based on 
sample group of 60 users, revealed that the mean satisfaction rating Section 1: Overall 
satisfaction with the website was 4.37 (or 87.48%), with a standard deviation of 0.57. Section 
2: Satisfaction with the financial planning content was 4.30 (or 86.00%), with a standard 
deviation of 0.61, which is considered the highest level of satisfaction. Furthermore,
